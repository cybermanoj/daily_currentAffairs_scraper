Amendments to the Immigration and Foreigners Rules, 2025

★ ★ ☆ ☆ ☆

Source: IE

The Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) has notified crucial amendments to the Immigration and Foreigners Rules, 2025 , tightening the registration timeline for foreign nationals seeking stay extensions and revising citizenship regulations for minors.

  • Revised Registration Timeline: Foreigners arriving in India on a visa of 180 days or less who intend to extend their stay must now register "any time before the expiry of the 180-day period" , replacing the earlier window that allowed registration within 14 days after the expiry of 180 days.
  • Stricter Norms for Multi-Entry Visas: Foreigners holding visas valid for more than 180 days, but with the condition that each stay should not exceed 180 days, must also register before the 180-day limit if they wish to stay longer. Such permission will now be granted only in emergent circumstances.
  • Relief for Newborns of Mixed Parentage: The mandatory 30-day electronic intimation rule to the registration officer upon the birth of a child to foreign nationals will no longer apply if one of the parents is an Indian citizen who wishes to retain the child’s Indian citizenship.
  • Delayed Foreign Citizenship Clause: If a child of mixed parentage acquires foreign citizenship at a later stage while in India , the parents are legally obligated to inform the registration officer within 30 days of acquiring such foreign citizenship .
  • Medical Institutional Compliance: The notification has updated the administrative reporting requirements and compliance procedures for all hospitals, nursing homes, and medical facilities that provide lodging or medical services to foreign nationals.
  • Broader Citizenship Framework: These changes follow internal security tracking metrics, including draft amendments to the Citizenship Rules, 2009 (requiring declarations regarding passports from Pakistan, Afghanistan, and Bangladesh) and the Citizenship (Amendment) Rules, 2026 , which introduced electronic Overseas Citizen of India (e-OCI) cards .

Mission Senehjori

★ ★ ☆ ☆ ☆

Source: PIB

Recently, the Union Minister for Development of North Eastern Region (MDoNER), along with the Assam Chief Minister, launched Mission “Senehjori” – Assam Muga Silk USP, a cluster-based initiative to transform the state’s Muga silk sector.

  • Muga silk is the world’s only naturally golden silk, obtained from the silkworm Antheraea assamensis , which feeds on Som and Soalu host plants.
  • It is closely tied to Assam’s cultural heritage and is India’s first GI -tagged silk , valued for its natural golden colour, durability, and suitability for luxury textile markets.

Mission Senehjori

  • About: Mission “Senehjori” is a cluster-based initiative for Assam’s Muga silk sector, launched by the Ministry of Development of Northeastern Region in collaboration with the Government of Assam, Central Silk Board and other stakeholders.
    • It will be implemented across key Muga-producing clusters in districts such as Jorhat, Sivasagar, Lakhimpur, Dhemaji, Dibrugarh, Tinsukia, Majuli and Sualkuchi.
  • Objective: To develop a premium, traceable, export-oriented, and globally competitive luxury silk ecosystem while strengthening Assam’s cultural economy.
  • Financial Outlay: Estimated investment of ₹396-411 crore over three years, including ₹136-151 crore from MDoNER.
  • Livelihood Importance: Supports nearly 2.6 lakh rearer and weaver families, aiming to enhance incomes and monetise the sector.
  • Value Chain: The mission will strengthen host-plant cultivation, silkworm seed production, reeling, weaving, branding, quality assurance, export promotion, digital traceability, and tourism.
  • Institutional Support: Promotion of Farmer Producer Organisations (FPOs) , Common Facility Centres (CFCs), Farmer Interest Groups, GI authentication, and global market access under the unified “Senehjori” brand.
  • Targets by 2028: Establish five modernised Muga reeling units and a Muga Spun Mill, create 30 FPOs and over 1,180 Farmer Interest Groups, regenerate 5,000 hectares of Som and Soalu host plants, and GI-authenticate over 80% of traded Muga silk with digital traceability.
  • Silk Tourism: Development of a Muga Silk Trail, Silk Tourism Park, and annual Muga Utsav festivals to promote Assam’s silk heritage and cultural economy.

V. V. S. Aiyar

★ ★ ☆ ☆ ☆

Source: PIB

The Vice President of India paid homage to the great freedom fighter, revolutionary, and Tamil scholar Varahaneri Venkatesa Subramaniam Aiyar (V. V. S. Aiyar) on his death anniversary (3 rd June).

  • Early Life & Transition: Born in Tiruchirappalli (2 nd April 1881), he travelled to London in 1907 to study law but decisively shifted his path to become a committed revolutionary.
  • India House & Savarkar: In London, he became a core member of India House (founded by Shyamji Krishna Varma ) and worked closely with Vinayak Damodar Savarkar in the secret Abhinav Bharat Society , supporting revolutionary activities and facilitating the clandestine movement of arms into India.
  • Pondicherry Trio: To evade British intelligence, he escaped to the French territory of Pondicherry in 1910 , where he formed a formidable intellectual and revolutionary network alongside nationalist poet Subramania Bharati and spiritual leader Aurobindo Ghosh .
  • The Ashe Assassination (1911): Historically recognized for his role as a mentor, he secretly provided arms training to the young revolutionary Vanchinathan , who subsequently assassinated the tyrannical British Collector of Tirunelveli, Robert Ashe .
  • Journalism & Mainstream Politics: During World War I, he aligned closer to the Indian National Congress , edited the Tamil daily Desabhaktan , and was ultimately imprisoned for sedition in 1921 .
  • Nationalist Convictions: Despite qualifying as a barrister, Aiyar refused the title as it required an oath of allegiance to the British Crown.
  • Ideological Shift: Described by Mahatma Gandhi as a "fierce anarchist" in 1907, Aiyar later embraced the path of Ahimsa (non-violence) .
  • Literary Pioneer: Widely regarded as the Father of the Tamil Short Story, he pioneered modern Tamil storytelling through his collection Mangayarkkarasiyin Kaadhal.
    • His literary legacy is particularly associated with Kulathangarai Arasamaram , a celebrated short story from this collection that is considered a landmark in Tamil literature.
  • Classical Translations: He helped globalize Tamil literature by translating the Thirukkural and Kamba Ramayanam into English, and supervised the English translation of Savarkar's The Indian War of Independence, 1857 .
    • Aiyar deeply admired Kamban , the renowned author of the Kamba Ramayanam , and regarded him as superior to classical Sanskrit poets Valmiki and Vyasa .
  • Biographies of Revolutionaries: To inspire Indian youth, he authored Tamil biographies of global leaders like Giuseppe Garibaldi, Guru Gobind Singh, and Napoleon.
  • Nationalist Education: Rejecting the British system, he established the Bharadwaja Ashrama , a Tamil Gurukulam in Cheranmahadevi in 1922 , focused on cultural and vocational education.
  • Tragic End: His life abruptly ended on 3 rd June 1925 when he drowned in the Papanasam falls while attempting to save his daughter.

Quark-Gluon Plasma

★ ★ ☆ ☆ ☆

Source: TH

Researchers at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) have discovered the smallest known droplet of Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) that still exhibits fluid-like behaviour, challenging existing understandings of how matter behaves at the subatomic level.

  • Understanding QGP: Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) is an extremely hot state of matter made up of quarks and gluons , the basic building blocks of matter.
    • A QGP is a dense state of matter that existed during the first few millionths of a second after the Big Bang , prior to the formation of protons and neutrons.
    • QGP is one of the hottest substances ever created , reaching temperatures of trillions of degrees Celsius .
    • Scientists create QGP by smashing atomic nuclei together at very high speeds inside the LHC .
    • QGP was mainly observed in collisions involving heavy elements such as lead .
  • Fluidity Paradox: Despite consisting of only thousands of subatomic particles compared to the quadrillions of molecules found in macroscopic fluids like water, QGP behaves like a near-perfect fluid with properties like viscosity and flow, rather than acting like a gas.
  • Oxygen Collision Breakthrough: Scientists collided oxygen nuclei in LHC and found that even this much smaller system could create QGP.
    • Oxygen serves as a crucial middle ground between light protons and heavy lead nuclei , helping locate the tipping point where independent particles transition into a collective fluid.
  • Evidence of Quark Energy Loss: Scientists have discovered the smallest-ever QGP that still behaves like a liquid instead of a gas.
    • They found that particles moving through QGP lose energy, showing that it acts as a dense fluid.
    • This discovery helps scientists understand how matter changes from a gas-like state to a liquid-like state at the smallest scales .

Large Hadron Collider (LHC)

  • The LHC, located at the CERN (European Laboratory for Particle Physics) laboratory near Geneva, Switzerland, is the world's largest and most powerful particle accelerator .
  • Consisting of a 27-kilometre underground ring of superconducting magnets , it accelerates beams of protons or heavy ions to nearly the speed of light before smashing them together.
  • These extremely high-energy collisions recreate the exact physical conditions that existed fractions of a second after the Big Bang , allowing physicists to observe how subatomic particles interact.
  • Most famous for enabling the 2012 discovery of the Higgs boson (the particle responsible for giving other particles mass), the LHC continues to be at the forefront of modern physics, helping scientists unravel the mysteries of dark matter, antimatter, and exotic states of matter like quark-gluon plasma.
  • India–CERN Cooperation: Under a 1996 Department of Atomic Energy (DAE)–CERN protocol , India joined the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) project, contributing hardware, software, and skilled manpower.
