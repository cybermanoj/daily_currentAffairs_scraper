India Re-included in EU's List for Aquaculture Export

★ ★ ☆ ☆ ☆

Source: PIB

India has been re-included in the European Union 's revised draft list for continued export of aquaculture products, ensuring uninterrupted seafood flow to one of its largest global markets. It addresses the previous exclusion of India in Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2598 .

  • Regulatory Compliance: The re-inclusion follows India's strict adherence to Delegated Regulation (EU) 2023/905 , which mandates that animal products must be free from antimicrobials used for growth promotion or those reserved for human treatment .
  • India's compliance is supported by robust systems like the National Residue Control Programme (NRCP) and the Post Harvest Testing Programme , which monitor for banned antibiotics and pharmacologically active substances.
  • The development also recognises sustained efforts undertaken by the Department of Commerce through organisations such as the Marine Products Export Development Authority (MPEDA) and the Export Inspection Council (EIC) to strengthen regulatory compliance and promote responsible aquaculture practices .
  • Economic Significance: The EU is India's 3 rd -largest seafood market , accounting for 18.94% of total export value (USD 1.593 billion) in 2025–26, with farmed shrimp remaining the primary export commodity.

Aquaculture Scenario in India

  • Global & Economic Standing: India is the 2 nd largest fish-producing nation in the world after China, accounting for approximately 8% of global output . The fisheries sector accounts for nearly 7.43% of the Agricultural Gross Value Added (GVA) .
    • Seafood exports reached a record Rs 62,408 crore in FY 2024–25. Frozen shrimp remains the dominant export item, with high demand from the US and China.
  • Production Dynamics: India's fish production nearly doubled over the last decade, reaching 197.75 lakh tonnes in 2024–25. Inland production now accounts for roughly 75% of total fish production.
    • Andhra Pradesh is the largest producer followed by West Bengal , Uttar Pradesh , and Gujarat .
  • Key Government Schemes: Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana , Fisheries & Aquaculture Infrastructure Development Fund , Pradhan Mantri Matsya Kisan Samridhi Sah Yojana etc.

Singapore as India's 2nd-Largest Export Market

★ ★ ☆ ☆ ☆

Source: IE

The prolonged West Asia war has significantly altered India’s trade and energy supply chains, with Singapore overtaking UAE as India’s second-largest export destination amid severe disruptions in Gulf trade routes.  The US remains India's largest export destination .

  • Geopolitical Disruption in Trade: The blockage of the Strait of Hormuz (a critical maritime choke point between Iran and Oman) has forced Indian traders to find alternative logistics routes, severely disrupting traditional trade with the West Asia region.
    • The Strait previously accounted for a fifth of global oil flows .
  • Surge in Singapore Exports: Leveraging its status as a transshipment hub and its Free Trade Agreement (FTA) with India, exports to Singapore surged by 180% .
    • Conversely, exports to the UAE (another major FTA partner) dropped by 36%. Diversification of Energy Imports: With traditional Middle Eastern supplies restricted, India has diversified its crude sourcing, bringing nations like Oman, Peru, and Nigeria into its top 20 import sources.
  • Macroeconomic Strain on India: The global energy supply crisis has widened India's import bill and severely impacted the currency, causing the Indian rupee to fall to multiple record lows (depreciating by 5.2% against the US dollar since February 2026).
    • To manage foreign exchange reserves and limit fuel consumption, the government has hiked the import duty on precious metals and allowed oil marketing companies to increase retail petrol and diesel prices for the first time in four years.

India- Singapore Trade and Economic Cooperation

  • Trade Dynamics: Driven by the Comprehensive Economic Cooperation Agreement (CECA) , bilateral trade expanded dramatically from USD 6.7 billion in FY 2004-05 to USD 34.3 billion in 2024-25 , solidifying Singapore as India’s 6th largest trading partner .
    • Singapore stands as India's largest source of FDI (USD 14.94 billion in FY 2024-25).
  • Institutional Frameworks: To actively facilitate cross-border capital, Invest India opened a dedicated office in Singapore in September 2024, complemented by the Singapore Business Federation establishing its maiden Indian office in Bengaluru in November 2025.
  • Pioneering Fintech Integration: Bilateral financial infrastructure achieved a global benchmark via the UPI-PayNow Linkage , establishing India's first operational cross-border Person-to-Person (P2P) payment facility .
  • Digital Commerce and Trade Finance: Strategic collaborations include the ONDC-Proxtera Connectivity for international retail applications, the GIFT Connect linkage (unifying liquidity pools between NSE and SGX for NIFTY products), and the TradeTrust architecture enabling interoperable electronic Bills of Lading (eBLs) .
